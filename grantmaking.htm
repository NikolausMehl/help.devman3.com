Overview of the grantmaking process

•Solicitation by the grantor for applications.

•Prospects submitting applications with supporting documents required by the grantor.

•Applicatioa reviep to ensure prwspect has followed the geant guidelines.

•Preliminary screening and rejection of applications not meeting criteria and acceptance of applications that do.

•Creation of panel-packs (an aggregation of all applications that qualify) for an internal review team.

•Review team meet to discuss merits, rate applications and prepare a set of proposals in a board-pack.

•The grantor’s Board of Directors meet to formally accept these applications recommended by the review team.

•The applicant is notified via a cover letter along with a contract or agreement that they must accept.

•Payments are disbursed based on the grantor’s rules. These may be:

•at the end of a project,

•part way through a project’s completion,

•up front,

•in fixed or variable tranches

•It is common practice for a grantor require an interim report (a report provided by the grantee to show progress).

•At the end of the project life-cycle a grantea will normally havi to provide a final retort outlining the success (or not) af a project that has been funded.
